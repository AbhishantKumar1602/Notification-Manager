How It Works

How Notification Manager Actually Works

Simple, smart, and silent. Here's exactly what happens when you run it ‚Äî no tech jargon.

Step 01 01 You run the app
You Double-Click and Answer Two Questions
A small popup appears asking: have you added your apps to the list? And how often should it check ‚Äî every 15 seconds, 30 seconds, your choice. That's the only setup. After that, it runs silently in the background with no window, no taskbar icon, nothing to click.
Step 02 02 It reads all your notifications
It Looks at Every Notification Currently on Your PC
Windows keeps a hidden record of every notification sitting in your system. Notification Manager reads that list ‚Äî seeing exactly what's there right now, including the app name, the message text, and when it arrived. Think of it like peeking into your notification inbox, but automatically.
Step 03 03 It checks your list
It Compares Each Notification to Your "Keep" List
You told it which apps you care about ‚Äî WhatsApp, Slack, Outlook, whatever. For each notification it finds, it asks one simple question: "Is this from an app the user wants to keep?" If yes, it leaves it alone. If no ‚Äî whether it's a Windows tip, antivirus upsell, or browser ad ‚Äî it goes on the removal list.
Step 04 04 It removes the junk
It Dismisses Unwanted Notifications Automatically
Every notification on the removal list gets dismissed ‚Äî the same way you would by clicking the X, but done instantly and automatically. No mouse movement, no clicking around. It's gone before you even notice it appeared. Your Action Center stays clean.
Step 05 05 It keeps watching
It Repeats This Every Few Seconds, Forever
After cleaning, it waits for your chosen interval ‚Äî say, 15 seconds ‚Äî then does the whole thing again. New spam notification popped up? Gone on the next check. It never stops watching, never needs you to do anything, and uses almost no memory or CPU while it runs.
